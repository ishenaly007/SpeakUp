<div id="lesson-art-of-storytelling" class="lesson-content">
  <h1>The Timeless Art of Storytelling</h1>
  <p class="intro">Since the dawn of humanity, stories have been the way we share our history, our dreams, and our very essence. Let's delve into the core elements that make a story resonate.</p>

  <section id="story-plot">
    <h2>The Plot: What Happens?</h2>
    <p>The plot is the sequence of events in a story. A well-crafted plot keeps the reader engaged and eager to know what's next. Common plot structures include:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>The Hero's Journey:</strong> A classic structure where the protagonist goes on an adventure, faces a crisis, and returns transformed.</li>
      <li><strong>Freytag's Pyramid:</strong> Exposition, Rising Action, Climax, Falling Action, and Denouement.</li>
    </ul>
    <blockquote>
      "The king died and then the queen died is a story. The king died and then the queen died of grief is a plot." - E.M. Forster
    </blockquote>
  </section>

  <section id="story-characters">
    <h2>Characters: Who Drives the Story?</h2>
    <p>Characters are the heart of any story. Whether heroes, villains, or something in between, compelling characters are relatable and memorable.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Protagonist:</strong> The main character, often the one the audience roots for.</li>
      <li><strong>Antagonist:</strong> The character or force that opposes the protagonist.</li>
      <li><strong>Supporting Characters:</strong> Characters who add depth and help move the plot forward.</li>
    </ul>
    <div class="character-spotlight">
      <img src="placeholder_wise_mentor.png" alt="Placeholder for a wise mentor character" />
      <p>Think of iconic characters like Sherlock Holmes or Elizabeth Bennet. What makes them unforgettable?</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="story-setting">
    <h2>Setting: Where and When?</h2>
    <p>The setting establishes the time and place of the story. It can be as crucial as a character, influencing mood and events.</p>
    <p>Consider a futuristic city versus a medieval castle. How does the setting change the story's possibilities?</p>
  </section>

  <section id="story-theme">
    <h2>Theme: The Underlying Message</h2>
    <p>The theme is the central idea or insight the story explores. It's what the story is 'about' on a deeper level.</p>
    <p>Themes can be universal, such as love, loss, courage, or the struggle between good and evil.</p>
  </section>

  <footer>
    <p>Start weaving your own tales today!</p>
  </footer>
