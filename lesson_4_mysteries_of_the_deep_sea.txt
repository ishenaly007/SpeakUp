    <h2>Hydrothermal Vents: Oases of Life</h2>
    <p>Discovered in 1977, hydrothermal vents are like underwater geysers, spewing superheated, mineral-rich water from beneath the Earth's crust. These vents support unique ecosystems based on chemosynthesis, where bacteria convert chemicals into energy, forming the base of a food web that includes giant tube worms, crabs, and shrimp.</p>
